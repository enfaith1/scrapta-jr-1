What is E-Waste?

E-waste is short for electronic waste. It's all the old gadgets and devices that we don't use anymore, like broken phones, old computers, and worn-out TVs.

Examples of E-Waste

  • Old cell phones and tablets
  • Broken laptops and computers
  • Discarded chargers and cables
  • Outdated TVs and game consoles

Why Does E-Waste Matter?

E-waste can be harmful to our planet if not handled properly. It contains chemicals that can pollute the air, water, and soil. But when we recycle it, we can turn it into new things and keep the Earth clean!

Fun fact: Recycling one million laptops saves enough energy to power 3,500 homes for a year!
